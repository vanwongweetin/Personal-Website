Vanessa Wong

Through this portfolio, I want to show my viewers that I have fun and creative ideas when designing a website and can develop an interesting and responsive website. I enjoy choosing colours that fit the given theme and coming up with creative ideas that can “wow” the audience. Additionally, after having developed a website I wish to develop and pleasing my client, I love the feeling of satisfaction and achievement in the end.

I hope that the people who reach my portfolio are those who are looking for someone who has a creative mind and can design and develop a fun website of their wish.

I have designed and developed a website where viewers can easily navigate throughout the website via the navigation bar at the top. Additionally, the contents of each page are placed in the middle of the screen, where most of the contents are visible to the viewers immediately after entering the page, saving them the trouble of having to scroll to find the information they are looking for.

Accessibility

#1 Use of "alt" attributes

Firstly, the site uses alternative text (alt attributes) for all images which is useful for the visually impaired who are browsing the site and rely on screen readers. The format I used to label the images in the alt attribute is like so - *description of image* image. Hence, when the screen reader reads it, the listener would know that there is an image and what the image is showing.


#2 Use of semantic HTML5 tags

Secondly, the site employs semantic HTML5 tags, like “nav”, “main” and “footer”. These tags will help assistive technologies, like screen readers, better understand and navigate the structure of the web pages, providing a more accessible browsing experience.
For example, users are able to skip to the main content or find the navigation menu with ease.


#3 Clear & Consistent navigation and tab bars

Thirdly, the use of a consistent and easy-to-understand navigation bar lets viewers with cognitive disabilities quickly and easily understand and familiarise themselves with the navigation bar and tabs that are present on some pages, allowing them to navigate the site effectively. The navigation bar and tabs are also fixed at the top of the screen and in the pages respectively. Additionally, the background of the active page in the navigation bar would be a different colour from the rest of the pages, allowing viewers of the site to straight away know which page they are on.



Usability

#1 Intuitive Navigation

The website uses a clear menu structure ensuring the navigation menu is straightforward and organized, allowing viewers of the website to find information quickly. Descriptive labels are used for links and menus to make navigation intuitive. A consistent layout is maintained across all pages, so viewers know where to find navigation elements and content. This consistency helps them feel comfortable and confident as they browse the site. Additionally, the navigation bar and tabs use the same CCS format, which is having a font colour for the active page and tab while not having a colour for the inactive pages and tabs but instead outline (stroke).
For example, if the viewer of the site is on the experience page, the word “Experience” on the navigation bar would stand out from the other words/pages by having a font colour and a different background colour. Additionally, if the viewer is looking at my education details, the “Education” tab would have a font colour as it is the active tab while the “Work” tab would not have a font colour.


#2 Responsive Design

The navigation bar and tabs present on some of the pages can be navigated via the Tab and Enter keys, making the site accessible to viewers with motor disabilities or viewers who just prefer to browse the site using their keyboard. I had designed the user interface to display most, if not all, of the contents of the page on the screen without needing viewers of the site to have to scroll further down to move more. This allows the viewer to be able to navigate throughout the website and toggle the tabs using the Tab and Enter keys.
For example, on the “Experience” page, all education and work experience are displayed on the page without having to scroll up and down or left and right. Viewers would just have to toggle between the “Education” and “Work” tabs and they can do so with the Tab and Enter keys.


#3 Sufficient Contrast

While backgrounds have light colours, all fonts have either a dark colour or outline (stroke) to make the text stand out, making it easy for, not just viewers of the website with visual impairments, like colour blindness or low vision, but all viewers to read them. Additionally, alternating between the two background colours makes the pages look less dull.
For example, the main background colours are light blue for the body and light yellow for the navigation bar. However, having only light blue for the body throughout the website can be boring and grey. Therefore, I decided to make the music player on the “About” page and the sections on the “Experience” and “Project” page light yellow, the colour of the navigation bar.



Learning

#1 Transition & Animation

I decided to research and apply transitions and animations to my website to make it feel more fun. I felt that adding animations to the main content of every page and hover transitions to clickable links gives life to the website and makes the user experience less boring for viewers of the website. After much research on YouTube and W3Schools and trying and experimenting with different types of animations and transitions, I was finally able to choose the type of animation and transition that best fit my website.
I decided to give the pages a moving animation so that the text of the main content of each page would start slightly to the left (viewers’ right) of its original position and then smoothly slide to its original position, which is the middle of the page for the Home, About, and Contact pages and the middle of the box/container it is in for the Experience, and Project page.
As for the transition, I picked one to implement in the CSS of my website, more specifically, for the hover action. For the navigation bar and tabs in the Experience and Project pages, inactive pages and tabs would have no fill and only have an outline. Hovering over them would give them a fill that would appear with an ease-out transition. On the Home page, there are links that direct to the About, Project and Report pages. These links when hovered over will increase in front weight and will be underlined and these changes will appear with an ease-out transition too. Lastly, on the Contact page, when hovering over the “Send Message” button, a shadow around the button would appear, once again, with an ease-out transition.


#2 Music Player

With the help of a tutorial on YouTube on how to develop the music player, I learnt how to incorporate audio into my website. Additionally, as I had coded the music player separately in another folder before shifting it to my website, there were many “position”, “top”, “left” and “right” properties I had to edit to fit the music player nicely into the “About” page. It was my first time using those properties, hence, I had to do more research on them to know how they work.
I struggled with the JavaScript part of the music player the most as I followed the tutorial closely, but my music player would sometimes not work like the one in the tutorial. As I used different names for the class attributes from the tutorial video, it was harder to spot the parts where I coded wrongly. After going back and forth with the tutorial video, my music player was finally working as I wanted it to.
However, as mentioned before, I coded the music player on another folder first before shifting it to the actual folder of my website after it was working perfectly, this led to properties in my website code and music player code to clash and not work well together. The song image would appear out of the music image box and the text in the music player would be hidden behind the song image. To counter this CSS issue, I looked through the music player CSS code line by line and made edits to fit the music player into the About page. What is seen in the current About page is the perfectly integrated music player.


#3 Media Queries

Making a website responsive was something new to me, hence, I had to research and learn about ways to make a website responsive to various screen sizes. I thought the topic would be difficult for me to get a grasp on, however, thanks to a YouTube tutorial about CSS media query, within minutes, I could understand how I can make my website responsive with media queries.
Getting to know the basics was easy, but I had to figure out how I could include media queries into my code. While implementing them into my code, at first I felt a little stressed and confused as the content decreased in size when I did not want them to be and increased in size when I did not want them to. After much guess-and-check, I was finally able to get a hang of using media queries and my pages are now responsive and can fit into various screen sizes.
Although the current look does not look the best, I am satisfied with how it turned out considering it was my first time working with media queries. I do plan to study more on it so that the websites that I will be developing in the future will be even more responsive and professional.

Evaluation II

#1 Animation

The only animation I have on this website is the main content text sliding into the middle of the webpage. #2 Media Queries

As mentioned in “Learning”, CSS media query is something new to me. Hence, I am not yet very familiar with it, nor have I discovered the full potential of it. After this assignment, I plan to do more research on media queries and try using it more often to expand my use of it. After applying media queries to make this website responsive, even though it works, the site still looks weird when the screen is resized. Additionally, items that are too fixed together, like the music player on the About page, are confusing and difficult for me to understand how I can resize each part of them one by one. In the future when I add media queries to my website, I want to better implement it so that my website will look great on any screen size.
